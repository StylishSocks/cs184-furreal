Title: FurReal: Interactive Real-Time Hair and Fur Simulation

Summary: Hair and fur are visually important but difficult to simulate in real time because each strand needs to remain inextensible while also responding to motion, collision, and external forces. In this final project, we will build an interactive real-time hair and fur simulation system that supports thousands of strands, approximate hair-hair interactions, and user-controlled effects such as wind and styling. Our goal is to create a simulation that is physically plausible and visually compelling for animated characters.

Problem Description: 

Realistic hair and fur are still challenging problems in computer graphics, especially in interactive settings. Unlike offline film production, where simulation can afford to take significant time, real-time applications such as games/demos require stable and visually believable motion under strict performance constraints. Hair and fur are difficult to simulate because they consist of thousands of thin strands, each of which need to resist stretching, respond to gravity, and interact with the body and neighboring hairs.

Our project focuses on building a real-time physical simulation system for hair and fur attached to an animated character or model. We are interested in strand-based simulation methods that maintain inextensibility while remaining efficient enough for interactive framerates. One inspiration for our project is Müller et al.’s “Fast Simulation of Inextensible Hair and Fur,” which presents an approach for simulating thousands of strands with a single iteration per frame, along with approximate methods for hair-hair repulsion, friction, and character collision.

The main challenge is balancing realism, robustness, and performance. A physically accurate simulation of every hair-hair interaction is too computationally expensive as the number of strands increases. Similar to what is currently done, we plan to use efficient approximations such as particle chains for strands, simplified collision volumes for the character, and density-grid-based approximations for hair-hair interaction. 

Our general approach is to represent each hair strand as a chain of particles connected by distance constraints. We will implement a Follow-The-Leader style solver to enforce strand inextensibility efficiently, then add collision handling, external forces, and interactive controls such as brushing or wind. We also plan to support multiple visual styles, including short fur and longer styled hair, to demonstrate the flexibility of our system.


What we plan to deliver: 
Baseline Goals
We will build a working real-time strand-based simulation system for hair and fur that demonstrates stable motion, plausible visual behavior, and interactive performance.
This will include:
Core Simulation
A strand-based hair/fur model where each strand is represented as a chain of particles.
We will preserve strand length and minimize visible stretching.
Gravity, inertia, and damping for dynamic motion
Roots attached to a moving character head or body.

Simulation Modeling
External Forces: gravity, wind
Shape Matching
Static Root, 
Equi-mass Particles in chain





Collision and Interaction
Collision handling between hair strands and a simplified character model, likely using spheres, capsules, or ellipsoids rather than full mesh collision.
At least one form of user interaction, such as wind, dragging, or a brushing tool.
External force controls that allow us to demonstrate the responsiveness of the simulation live.


Rendering and Demo
A real-time rendering system that visualizes dense hair or fur clearly.
Implement Kajiya–Kay Model
Support for at least two distinct visual presets, such as short fur and long hair
An interactive demo scene in which a user can move the character or apply forces and observe the hair respond in real time
Evaluation
Performance: We will measure the frames per second as a function of strand count and particles per strand, targeting a minimum of 30 FPS with at least 1,000 strands at 10 particles per strand on CPU
We can plot FPS vs. strand count to help us identify our performance ceiling
Stability: Whether the simulation avoids visible stretching, jitter, or exploding motion
Visual quality: We will show qualitative comparisons showing the effect of enabling or disabling features such as collision, damping, and hair-hair interaction
Questions to Answer
We plan to deliver answers to:	
How does simulation performance scale with increasing strand count and segment resolution?
How well does our simulation preserve strand length under large motion?
We hope to deliver answer to:
How much do simplified collision and bulk interaction models improve visual realism?
Can the method generalize to different fur types (varying lengths, densities)?

Stretch Goals (Hope to Deliver)

Method comparison: We would like to compare our inextensible solver against a simpler based baseline to show differences in stretching, stability, and runtime

Resources:
Research Papers
Müller, Kim, and Chentanez, “Fast Simulation of Inextensible Hair and Fur”
https://matthias-research.github.io/pages/publications/FTLHairFur.pdf
Illuminates a geometrically-based method for efficiently simulating hair and fur in real time, maintaining the inextensibility of hair strands by representing each strand as a chain of fixed-length segments and updating particle positions using a Follow-The-Leader approach. This method propagates constraints from root to tip, in one pass, providing fast and stable simulation for interactive settings.
Müller, Hennix, and Ratcliff, “Position Based Dynamics”
https://matthias-research.github.io/pages/publications/posBasedDyn.pdf 
Showcases a Position Based Dynamics (PBD) framework that simulates particle systems by directly projecting positions to handle collision constraints.
Ward et al., “A Survey on Hair Modeling: Styling, Simulation, and Rendering”
https://gamma.cs.unc.edu/HAIRSURVEY/latest.pdf 
Extensive overview of techniques for creating, simulating, and rendering hair, providing good baselines. 
Petrovic, Henne, and Anderson, “Volumetric Methods for Simulation and Rendering of Hair”
https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Volumetric-Methods-for-Simulation-and-Rendering-of-Petrovic-Henne/8ba4778cc8b775d9940834a7328ce33637d8bafd
Presents techniques that model hair as a volumetric field, rather than a massive collection of individual strands, enabling efficient simulation and rendering of dense hair/fur.
Slides: Realtime Hair simulation & rendering
